Identity and values

Today we dug deep into ethical decisions and the clash of values that often accompany them.

An interesting example was my recent move into a new house in a townhouse development. With houses packed closer together, privacy becomes a luxury. So while the traditional Kiwi dream values independence, leaning into the community aspects of this new living arrangement opens up a wealth of positives. From semi-regular coffee catch-ups to home improvement tips (we are putting a ladder into our attic space) and shared meals, community living is proving to be a challenge worth embracing.

Culture and people around us significantly shape our values and identity. Independence, for instance, is a value instilled in me from a young age. Being part of a big family with a strong, solo mother has left a deep impression of resilience and autonomy.
