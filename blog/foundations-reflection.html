Human Skills in Software Development

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

Learning about human skills has given me a fresh perspective on my ego—essentially, my self-view. A standout moment was playing a values vs. values game using ChatGPT. It illuminated how I prioritize values like innovation over tradition, courage over prudence, and integrity over loyalty. Surprisingly, I found I value community over independence. As an introvert, this revelation was unexpected, yet it highlighted my appreciation for what can be achieved through collective effort, pushing me to prioritize building connections and community despite my natural tendencies.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Our values subtly guide our decisions, influencing the software and businesses we build. Being aware of these values allows us to consciously embrace or moderate them, ensuring our work positively impacts others. Empathy, on the other hand, helps us connect with our team and end-users, fostering effective collaboration and creating user-centric software. Self-awareness enhances this process, making us better team players and more mindful developers.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

Interestingly, I didn't find the human skills learning particularly surprising. At 40, having navigated numerous life experiences, I felt familiar with many concepts. However, the clarity these lessons provided was invaluable. For instance, during a recent argument, the looping exercises we learned came in handy. They helped de-escalate the situation by ensuring clear communication—an often overlooked yet crucial skill. Despite my initial skepticism, these exercises proved to be practical tools for improving interactions.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

The values section was particularly tough, primarily because I delved too deep. It's a vast topic, and while self-reflection is something I'm accustomed to, this exercise pushed me to explore even further. The depth of introspection required was both challenging and enlightening.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Focusing on human skills in a programming bootcamp is crucial for several reasons. First, collaboration is a core aspect of development. Whether working with fellow developers, designers, marketers, or open-source project committees, maintaining good working relationships is essential. Second, creating software that serves people effectively is central to good software development. Skills like communication, listening, and empathy are key to understanding and serving users, making human skills an integral part of the curriculum.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead?

No, the time spent on human skills is far from wasted. It's a crucial part of writing software that serves people effectively. However, I do have some reservations about the effectiveness of the exercises. Human skills are best learned through real-life challenges, conflicts, and compromises—situations that a controlled environment can't fully replicate. While I understand the importance of these lessons, I remain curious about their long-term impact. Nonetheless, I'm not an expert on teaching methodologies, so I'm open to seeing how these lessons will unfold in practice.
