Bootcamp Learning Plan

What is your long-term goal or career pathway?

My long-term goal is to either found a tech business or work in the tech industry. I am passionate about building various innovative projects and am equally enthusiastic about contributing to someone else’s vision. The key aspect of my career is to remain engaged and passionate about what I create; simply filling a seat without caring about the work is not an option for me. I don't have a fixed destination in mind, but I definitely see myself making software that helps people (ideally non-tech consumers). Whether it's productivity software or helpful apps, I aim to make people's lives easier.

What are your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning?

Several years ago, I took Gallup’s Strengthsfinder assessment and discovered that my strongest area is strategic thinking, followed by influencing and executing. My natural weakness lies in relationship building, though I recognize its value and strive to improve in this area. While empathy didn't rank highly in Strengthsfinder, I believe I have a significant capacity for caring about the end-user experience and eliminating small frustrations.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

My biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will likely be taking control during pair-programming exercises and listening to my partner's ideas. Recognizing the value of another perspective is essential, and I will do my best to manage this dynamic.

What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I aim to foster a greater sense of connectedness, appreciating others' perspectives and building upon them. The adage "a rising tide lifts all boats" resonates with me, emphasizing the importance of mutual support and growth. Better teamwork and communication are skills I am eager to develop.

How will you manage your workload in this program?

I have already made plans with my partner to carve out dedicated time for this course. Setting boundaries and ensuring downtime for recharging are crucial. I will manage my workload by balancing productivity with self-care, and collaborating effectively with fellow learners, facilitators, and industry/community representatives. When things become overwhelming, I will reassess my priorities and seek support as needed.

How (and where) will you seek help in a timely way?

While the Discord group is a valuable resource, I also look forward to having a supportive cohort at the Wellington office during the boot camp. We will be doing hybrid learning (part in-person, part remote). I aim to be present on Discord and in person to stay connected to the rest of my cohort.

What do you expect from the Facilitation team?

I expect the Facilitation team to be physically present in the Wellington office as much as possible. Their in-person support will significantly enhance our study experience and provide the necessary guidance.

Do you have any other commitments or scheduling conflicts to manage?

I have one major commitment: photographing a wedding in mid-July. However, I do not anticipate this affecting the boot camp phase of the program. Additionally, I need to consider transit to and from Lower Hutt, but I am already working on maintaining consistent and early wake-up and travel times to the city.
